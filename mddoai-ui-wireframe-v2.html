Sidebar : multi-tasking session tracker
Persistent on the left of every screen in the real app, shown here on 0a, a0, and d5 only to demonstrate the pattern rather than redrawn 38 times. Each tab has its own quick-action (+ New pipeline / + Add or Copy a platform) so starting new work never requires navigating away from whatever you're currently looking at first. Each open session gets its own named bar that stays until closed, stacking as more work starts. The session matching whatever's currently on screen gets a colored left edge (blue for pipelines, purple for platforms, on d5 that's the TeamCity bar), so you can tell which one you're inside. A bar shows a loading glyph while its generation is actively running, and its × is intentionally styled differently (muted red) from an idle session's plain ×, since closing a still-running one abandons in-progress work, not just dismisses a finished result. The panel's own background follows whichever mode (tab) is active, same blue/purple as everywhere else, and it has a small « Hide panel button (top-right of the sidebar) to push it out of the way.

Approved by uldiskk with 2 implementation notes not modeled further in this wireframe: (1) unlike every other screen here, the sidebar genuinely must stay visible across every panel in the real app, not just the 3 shown, that's a much bigger wireframe change than is worth doing by hand here, but don't forget it when building. (2) New users should land on a simplified first-run view, just the two main panels from 0a's loop-pill, no sidebar at all, plus an offered tutorial, rather than the full multi-session UI from day one, also not modeled as a separate screen here.
